Posted on Sun 03 April 2016 in Analysis

In case the title didn’t give it a way, this post relates to Game of Thrones, or more specifically to the series of books the show is based on: A song of Ice and Fire.

As anyone who watches the TV show or reads the books will know, this is a fairly bloody tale that involves a lot of characters dying. As a reader of the books, I couldn’t help but wander if there is some sort of pattern in the lifespan of characters in this series, so I decided to find out. Here’s the analysis.

I should begin by clarifying that when I refer to the life expectancy of characters, I don’t mean the average age at which characters die. I’m referring to the average time it takes, in number of chapters, between a character’s introduction and their death.
