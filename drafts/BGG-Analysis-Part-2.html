An analysis of board games: Part II - More on mechanics and themes

Posted on Sun 28 February 2016 in Analysis

This is part II in my series on analysing BoardGameGeek data. Other parts can be found here:

  • Part I: Introduction and general trends
  • Part II: More on mechanics and themes
  • Part III: Complexity bias in BGG

In Part I of this series, we looked at general trends in the release rate of board games, board game ratings, complexity, mechanics and themes have changed over the years. In this post, we’ll explore the co-occurance of mechanics and themes, as well as look at which mechanics/themes are associated with highly rated games.

Mechanics

Co-occurance

One of the things I was looking forward to when I got my hands on this data is to look at which mechanics co-occur most often. I think this list would not only be useful for game designers looking to see which additional mechanics they could incorporate into their game that fits with other mechanics in place, but more interestingly, it could be a challenge for designers to try to create interesting games that marry together mechanics seldom seen together. Let’s have a look at that this co-occurance table looks like.

From the matrix above, the mechanics that most often co-occur (relative to how much we’d expect them to co-occur by random distribution of mechanics, not in absolute terms), are shown in this table.

It seems like there’s a cluster of linked mechanics surrounding trading that dominate the most co-occuring mechanics list, but there’s also unsurprising combinations such as Singing with Acting, and Storytelling with role playing. Having had a look at the table, nothing stands out to me as being particuarly surprising, but it could still be a useful resource for board game designers.

Similarly, the mechanics that least often co-occur are shown below.

Mechanic 1 Mechanic 2 Correlation (R²)
Hand Management Roll / Spin and Move -0.18
Hex-and-Counter Roll / Spin and Move -0.14
Roll / Spin and Move Tile Placement -0.11
Set Collection Hex-and-Counter -0.11
Roll / Spin and Move Dice Rolling -0.11
Hex-and-Counter Hand Management -0.10
Dice Rolling Hand Management -0.10
Roll / Spin and Move Variable Player Powers -0.10
Simultaneous Action Selection Roll / Spin and Move -0.10
Area Control / Area Influence Roll / Spin and Move -0.09

These are all combinations of mechanics that are not seen together often. If any board game designers want a design challenge, it might be interesting to try to build a functional board game using a pair of these mechanics as the basis of the game. A particularly rare 3 mechanic combination is Roll / Spin and move, Hand management and Dice rolling, and a rare 4 mechanic combination is those 3 + Hex and Counter. I’d love to hear about how that experiment went if anyone actually decides to try to make a game out of these.

It’s worth noting that Roll / Spin and move comes up in 7 out of the 10 sets of least co-occurring mechanics. In fact, it’s occurance is negatively correlated with most other mechanics. The only mechanics that Roll / Spin and move has a positive correlation with are Trading, Auction/Bidding, Stock holding and Set collection.

This list of themes that don’t co-occur are slightly more interesting to me than the list of most co-occuring ones themes. It’s clear that Trivia games lend themselves to general knowledge, and as such there aren’t many specialist theme trivia games such as Fantasy trivia games, Fighting trivia games, Science fiction trivia games or World war II trivia games. Other themes that would be interesting to see together are Fantasy and Sports (would anyone like to try to make Quidditch: The board game?), Racing and Economic (I think Race for the Galaxy has this base covered), World war II games that incorporate Fantasy or Animals elements, Science fiction games that incorporate Animals as a theme and World war II Sports games (Out of all these least co-occurring mechanics, this is probably the pair that is understandable). If anyone is interested in trying to create an unusual and unique game, those are theme combinations worth trying.
